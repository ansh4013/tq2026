PixBit

Where Creativity Meets Pixels — topic and source images provided on the day.

Mode: Individual
Open To: All (Junior 5–8 & Senior 9–12)
Max Participants: 15
Duration: 3 Hours
Format: Computer-based
Topic: Announced on event day
Overview

PixBit is an individual design competition where participants use photo-editing tools to craft creative digital compositions. Each round presents a new challenge — from assembling image cutouts into logical scenes to designing full posters — culminating in a speed-based finale. All assets are provided; no external images or internet access is permitted.

Event Rounds
  • Round 1 — Pixel Summit (1 hr): Participants receive image cutouts and stickers and must arrange them into a logical, creative outcome. Top performers advance to Round 2.
  • Round 2 — Creative Fusion (1 hr): Participants design a poster on an assigned topic using provided images and stickers. Selected entries qualify for the Finale.
  • Finale — Mystery Asset & Speed Finale (1 hr): Participants receive hints describing the required outcome and must select the correct images/stickers to produce it. Winners are decided by how efficiently and accurately they complete the task.
Rules
  • Only provided images and stickers may be used — no external assets.
  • Internet access is strictly prohibited during the event.
  • The originality of each task must not be breached.
  • Any work targeting an individual, caste, religion, gender, or community will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Participation is by selection through a pre-event elimination round.
Judging Criteria
  • Creativity & Originality — imaginative and original use of provided assets.
  • Efficient Use of Provided Cutouts — thoughtful and purposeful selection of materials.
  • Color Combination & Effects — harmonious use of color and editing effects.
  • Overall Presentation & Visual Appeal — final quality and impact of the composition.
Submission
  • Save the final image to the Downloads folder.
  • Email it to techquestvsec@gmail.com within the 3-hour window.
  • Subject format: your Name, Class & Section, and Event Name.
  • File naming: name_class_eventname
